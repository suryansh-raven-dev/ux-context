# Product Archetypes

Raven ships three products today. They share the same component library but serve **different cognitive modes**. Treating them identically is wrong. Treating them as three separate design languages is also wrong.

They are **three shapes of the same language.**

## 2. Archetype definitions (binding)

Archetype **determines which patterns a product uses.** Every product MUST declare one archetype. A product MAY own sub-surfaces of a different archetype (for example, the Search canvas in P&amp;ID is a Conversational Canvas inside a Creative Canvas product), but the dominant archetype sets the **shell**, the **navigation model**, and the **default interaction grammar.**

### 2.1 Conversational Canvas

> Input is free-text intent. Output is streamed, cited, and multi-turn.

- **Input shape:** free-text intent.
- **Output shape:** streamed, cited, multi-turn responses.
- **Session:** history is persistent and auto-titled.
- **Optimized for:** recall and exploration.
- **Chrome:** quiet. Tenant branding is prominent.
- **Shell:** Wide Sidebar Shell (chat history lives in primary nav).
- **Primary product:** Raven Copilot.

### 2.2 Transactional Workflow

> Input is structured form data. Output is records with lifecycle state.

- **Input shape:** structured forms.
- **Output shape:** records with lifecycle state (In Progress → Closed, Released, Rejected, …).
- **Optimized for:** throughput, filterability, audit trail.
- **Chrome:** dense. Status is the primary semantic channel.
- **Shell:** Wide Sidebar Shell.
- **Primary product:** Near Miss Management System (NMMS).

### 2.3 Creative Canvas

> Input is direct manipulation on a spatial artifact. Output is persistent views of the artifact plus layered metadata.

- **Input shape:** select, pan, zoom, annotate on a spatial artifact.
- **Output shape:** persistent views plus layered metadata.
- **Optimized for:** comprehension of complex visuals.
- **Chrome:** collapses to rails to preserve canvas area.
- **Shell:** Rail Shell with Rail-with-Expand (a wide sidebar when expanded).
- **Primary product:** P&amp;ID Tool.

## 3. Product personalities

Each product MAY express a distinct flavor inside its archetype, but **personality MUST NOT override system tokens.** Personality is expressed through:

- **Default density** — Copilot: comfortable &middot; NMMS: dense &middot; P&amp;ID: dense with expand-on-demand
- **Default sidebar state** — Copilot: wide &middot; NMMS: wide &middot; P&amp;ID: rail, expandable
- **Iconography weight** — all three share `raven-ui` rounded icons; variant choice differs
- **Empty-state voice** — Copilot: inviting &middot; NMMS: instructional &middot; P&amp;ID: neutral

Personality **MUST NOT** be expressed through custom colors, custom typography, or forked components.

## 5. Picking an archetype for a new surface

1. **What is the user's primary input?** Free text → Conversational. Form fields → Transactional. Direct manipulation → Creative.
2. **What is the persistent artifact?** A session history → Conversational. A record with a lifecycle → Transactional. A spatial diagram → Creative.
3. **Where does the chrome need to go?** Can yield to output → Conversational. Must stay dense for scanning → Transactional. Must collapse to maximize stage → Creative.

If two archetypes both fit, the **dominant** one (what the user is doing 80% of the time on the surface) wins. Sub-surfaces of a different archetype are fine — Search inside P&amp;ID is a Conversational Canvas nested inside a Creative Canvas product, and that is by design.

---

## 6. Rule for frontend

- A product surface **MUST** declare its archetype in the file-level JSDoc or a README adjacent to the route.
- Components **MUST NOT** gate behavior on product slug; they **MAY** gate on archetype when the design spec calls for it.
- LLMs generating new surfaces **MUST** identify the archetype first, then pull the matching shell from Section 5 of the [source guidelines](../raven-design-system-guidelines.md).
